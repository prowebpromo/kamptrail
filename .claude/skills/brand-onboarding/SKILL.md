# Brand Onboarding Skill Summary

This document outlines a five-phase process for onboarding new clients into a social media content system.

## Core Purpose

The skill captures visual identity, content patterns, audience insights, and business objectives to create `context/brand-style.md`—a reference file enabling consistent AI-generated social content across multiple tools.

## Key Phases

**Phase 0** establishes project folders and checks for existing brand documentation.

**Phase 1** uses web scraping to gather evidence from the client's website and social profiles before asking questions, reducing their burden by pre-filling known information.

**Phase 2** generates a client-facing onboarding document with three sections: confirmed facts for review, genuine information gaps requiring client input, and asset requests (brand colours, fonts, logos, product photos).

**Phase 3** processes returned materials—organizing logos, product images, and responses into structured folders.

**Phase 4** synthesizes all inputs into the final brand style guide covering overview, audience, goals, colour palette, typography, signature post formats, content pillars, photography style, rules, formats, tone, and sample captions.

**Phase 5** presents the completed guide for client review and adjustment.

## Critical Success Factors

The process emphasizes capturing real caption examples and signature content formats as primary inputs, since these determine whether generated content matches the brand's actual voice and visual patterns rather than appearing generic.
